Why is Credit Score Important?

Maintaining or striving for an excellent credit score, goes along with budgeting. When
seeking a new apartment, house, car, loans, etc. your credit score is used to evaluate how reliable you
are when it comes to paying bills. A credit score ranges from 300 - 850. A score of 300 is considered bad credit
and a score of 850 is considered excellent credit. There are five components that make up your credit score, which
consist of payment history, credit usage, credit history, new credit, and credit mix.


Payment History

Payment history makes up 35% of your credit score and is responsible for making sure the credit card company being utilized
are aware of the time frame in which the payments are being made. It is important to pay your credit card debt and loan payments
on time because late payments cause you credit score go down. Credit card companies will beleive that you are unreliable and
cannot make payments on time.


Credit Usage

Credit Usage makes up 30% of your credit score and refers to a ratio of how much is being spent on each card
if you have more than one versus how much is available. For example, if you spent in total is $3,000 and the
avaliable credit is $9,000, your credit usage is 33%. It's best to keep your credit usage low and can keep it low
by paying monthly credit card bills fully.


Credit History

Credit history makes up 15% of your credit score. This makes the company aware of how far back you have had
credit accounts and loans. It is better to have a longer history to show the company that you are reliable when
it comes to making payments and so that you are eligible for bigger credit avaliablity.


New Credit

New Credit makes up 10% of your credit score and makes the credit card company aware of any new credit accounts
or loans that might have been opened within the same time frame. When seeing this, companies often get concerned
and worry that the customer will not be able to pay their payments on time.


Credit Mix

Credit mix also makes up 10% of your credit score and lists the different types of credit you have such as credit
account, student loans, car loans, etc. Having multiple types of debt can be overwhelming for both the customer and
the credit card company, however, credit card companies like to see that you are managing multiple types of payments
which shows them that you can manage money efficiently.
