Why is financial literacy so important?

By: Viannie Veras

A teenagers guide to financial success in the future by starting now. In this guide, it will
discuss not only the importance of financial literacy, but components that factor into it such as
credit score, budgeting, managing debt such as student loans, as well as what the top five banks have
to offer when it comes to starting your financial journey.


As teenagers and young adults, it is important to know why we should start our financial journey now.
When obtaining a job and earning money, most people like to spend the majority of that money to seek
acceptance of others by looking cool, but when entering adulthood and attending higher education, it
can become hard balancing life and what may come after it. Starting now can help with stablilty and
can help with avoiding the fear of what's next?


The Basics (Budgeting)

The basics of financial literacy begins with budgeting. Everything revolves around budgeting as it is
very useful for financial success. Starting with a budget helps manage your money efficiently and helps
plan towards a goal. First, you would consider how much you earn, and with that construct a list of expenses
and write out how much they cost. Then you would constuct another list of other expenses such as shopping and
entertainment, then strategize how much you are willing to spend, however still having money to save. After
determining how much you are spending on expenses, consider the surplus and set aside a good portion or what
ever you can to your savings. Try to keep budegting and saving a habit and keep it consistant.

Saving Money

Another aspect of financial literacy is saving money. Saving money is important when it comes to things like
emergenices and financial goals. Saving goes along with budegeting, it is something that needs to be a habit
and is something that needs to be consistant. With consistancy comes gains and accomplishments that will lead
you to your financial dreams. When deciding how much you should start saving for a particular goal, consider time,
budget, and surplus. While working toward a financial or any goal, considering time is crucial, as it determines
how long it takes until it reaches success. This helps with planning even bigger goals that may come up in the
future. With this, acknowledging a budget is also pivotal because a budget plans out your spendings. When having
a set plan on spendings, considering the surplus and decide how much is needed and how much can be put away
toward savings. Having a savings plan is very important because there will be times in life where you don't know
what may come next or what may know what may come next or what may come up and in those types of situations,
having money to be able to fall back on can be extremely helpful.

Retirement Plans

Along with budgeting, comes financial plans. One of the most important financial plans that should be considered
is your retirement plans. Normally, this plan is associated with the benefit of most jobs, as a certain percentage
of your paycheck is required to be put away towards your retirement. In most cases, the percentage deducted from
your pay check can be chosen. In doing so, you would want to make sure that the surplus of your paycheck will suffice
your expenses. If you have a job that does not provide the opportunity to use a percentage of your paycheck to save
money for retirement, then set up a savings account, specifically for retirement and everytime you get paid, set aside
a percentage of your paycheck or a set amount of money to save. With this, emsure that the money being taken out will
suffice expenses, as well as keeping the payment consistant to make sure the amount being saved, grows over time.

Managing Debt

When working toward a financial goal, such purchasing a house or car, there may be times where you may not have
the kind of money you would like and may need to take out a loan. Loans can be helpful with many things, however,
can become a problem if there is no management of the payments. Mismanagement of a loan can result is debt building
upon itself, which can contribute to decrease in credit score. It can also take a toll on mental and emotional health
as it can cause mental ilnesses such as depression and anxiety. Managing money all starts with budgeting and setting a
with the management of debt, expenses, and other financial plans. It is very important to make sure that before taking
out a loan, a plan is already set on how the debt will be paid off and how long it will take based off the amount being
planned to pay.

Top Three Budgeting Apps

1. Mint

Mint ia a free application in which allows users to sync their bank accounts to help manage their spendings and
earnings. This application provides users with categories such as transactions, bills, budget, goals, investments,
and different ways to save as well as reports on expenses such as debt, spendings, etc. to help with managing,
expenses.

When it comes to financial apps and putting sensitive information, many people don't rely on financial apps
due to fear of hackers obtaining their information. With Mint, they have the same level of security as a
financial institution and reportedly hire hackers to test the security, making sure the security is reliable. Their
in app security procedures consist of security questions, passwords, and touch id. It is important that before
applying sensitive information to any financial application, you check with your bank to be aware of
consequences when it comes to breaches and scams.

This application is available for IOS and Android.

2. You Need A Budget

You need a budget is an application made to help users work toward or manage a budget. Before even

financial success.

Touching upon security, their framework is bulit with Amazon Web Services which is the same services
in which government agencies such as the CIA uses. They also follow the bug bounty program in which
