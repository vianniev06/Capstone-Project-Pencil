Banking


Finding the right bank is crucial because at
the end of the day, that is where your money is going to go. Below, you will find information regarding the
top five banks and what they cater to when it comes to student banking such as checking and savings account.


Top Five Banks and Student Banking


1. JP Morgan Chase and Co.

Checking Accounts: Chase offers a checking account for high school students between ages 13-17. It allows students to
receive direct deposit with no monthly fee. This account is linked to a parent or guardian's existing chase account
as co-owner of the account. Chase also offers a college student account for students between the ages of 17-24 that
that is ismilar to the high school checking account, but for a older age group. In order to transition
from a high school checking account to a college checking account, the student would have to see a Chase banker and provide proof of
enrollment in higher education such as an acceptance letter, transcript, etc. After graduation,
the account will still function the same. However, the account will require a $6 monthly fee unless one of the
following takes place: direct deposit, or an average ending balance of $5,000.


Savings Accounts: Chase mainly offers two types of saving accounts.

Chase Savings Account: This savings account has a monthly fee of $0 or $5. This monthly fee can be waived if one
of the following take place: a daily minimum balance of $25-$300, auto savings, or other continuous transfers
from your personal checking account, if it is linked to a Chase Checking Account for overdraft protection, if it is linked to other checking
accounts, or if the owner is younger than the age of 18. Interest is compounded and credited monthly based on the daily
balance. All balances have a rate of 0.01% as well as the annual percentage yield.

Chase Premier Savings: This savings account has a monthly fee of $0 or $25. The monthly fee can be waived with one
of the following, daily minimum balance of $15,000 or more, or if the savings account is linked to a checking account.
Interest rates are based on the balance of the account and range from 0.01% to 0.05%.


2. Bank of America Corp.

Checking Accounts: Students under the age of 24 enrolled in school are eligible for two checking accounts. These
accounts are also eligible to waive the monthly maintainance fee.

Bank of America Advantage Safe Banking: This account includes keeping existing money presented available money and
allows customers to purchase items with a debit card or with any mobile or online banking with no monthly fee.

Saving Accounts: With a Bank of America savings account, a minimum of $100 is required to open an account. Monthly fees
can be avoided by maintaining a minimum balance of $500, linking the account to a Bank of America Advantage Relationship Banking
account, or if the owner is a student under the age of 24.


3. Wells Fargo and Co.

Checking Accounts: Wells Fargo provides an everyday checking account for students between the ages of 17-24. To open
an account, students are required to have a minimum of $25 and monthly fees will be waived. After the student passes the
age of 25, the account will be charged a monthly fee of $10 unless one of the following takes place: direct deposit
totaling over $500 or more, 10 or more posted debit card transactions, a $1,500 minimum daily balance or more, or a
linked Wells Fargo campus ATM, or campus debit card. As per the Wells Fargo website, after October 8, 2020, the 10
or more posted debit card transactions will no longer be an option to waive the monthly fee. Another change in their
site, is with the $1,500 minimum daily balance. After October 8, 2020, the $1500 will be lowered to $500.


Savings Accounts: Wells Fargo offers two main savings accounts.

Wells Fargo Way2Save Savings: This savings account requires a minimum of $25 along with a monthly fee of $25. The
monthly can be avoided by having a daily minimum of $300, one or more transfers from a checking account, automatic
transfer of $25 from a checking account, or the primary owner is under the age of 18. This account includes standard rates of 0.01%.

Platinum Savings: This account requires a minimum of $25 and a monthly fee of $12 that can be avoided by maintaining
a daily minimum of $3,500. This account includes standard rates ranging from 0.01% to 0.02%.


4. Citigroup/Citibank Inc.

Checking Accounts: For checking accounts, Citigroup/Citibank doesn't have a particular account that caters to students,
however, there are basic accounts that may be useful. With basic accounts, they do not have monthly fees.

Basic Banking Account: This account consists of unlimited check writing, seamless money movement, and free ATM access.


Savings Accounts: With this account, there is no minimum required when opening the account.
A savings account with Citigroup is opened when purchasing a package which includes the rate, balance, requirements,
benefits, and more. Includes easy transfers between accounts from Citigroup and non Citigroup accounts, has the option of
setting balances and deposit alerts, mobile check deposit, higher rates when the balance grows and the option of writing
checks.


5. U.S Bancorp

Checking Accounts: This bank offers online and mobile banking and does cater to student accounts. For some schools,
such as colleges and universities, are associated with this bank, which can link a student's identification that can
offer student cards to be able to be used around campus. With a checking account, they do require a $2.00 fee when wanting
to receive paper statements, however e statements are free. To open an account, students are required to
have a minimum of $25 and does not contain a monthly fee.
