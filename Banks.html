Banking

When it comes to managing money, the role of a bank comes in. Finding the right bank is crucial because at
the end of the day, that is where your money is going to go. Below, you will find information regarding the
top five banks and what they cater to when it comes to student banking such as checking and savings account.

Top Five Banks and Student Banking

1. JP Morgan Chase and Co.

Checking Accounts: Chase offers a checking account for high school students between ages 13-17. It allows students
to receive direct deposit with no monthly fee. This account is linked to a parent or guardian's existing chase account
as co-owner of the account. Chase also offers a college student account for students between the ages of 17-24 that
allows the same thing as a high school checking account, but for a older age group. When it comes to transitioning
from a high school checking to a college checking, the student would have to see a Chase banker and with proof of
enrollment of high education such as acceptance letter, transcript, etc. After graduation or after the monthly waiver,
the account will still finction the same, however, the account will be moved to the $6 monthly fee unless one of the
following takes place, direct deposit, or an average ending balance of $5,000.


Savings Accounts: Chase mainly offers two types of saving accounts.

Chase Savings Account: This savings account has a monthly fee of $0 or $5. This monthly fee can be waived if one
of the following take place, a daily minimum balance of $300, $25 or more auto save, or other continuous transfers
from personal checking account, linked to Chase Checking Account for overdraft protection, linked to other checking
accounts, or if the owner is younger the age of 18. Interest is compounded and credited monthly based on the daily
balance. All balances have a rate of 0.01% as well as the annual percentage yield.

Chase Premier Savings: This savings account has a monthly fee of $0 or $25. The monthly fee can be waived with one
of the following, daily minimum balance of $15,000 or more, or if savings account is linked to checking accounts.
Interest rates are based on the balance of the account and range from 0.01% to 0.05%.


2. Bank of America Corp.

Checking Accounts: Students under the age of 24 enrolled in school are eligible for two checking accounts. These
accounts are also eligible for a waiver of monthly maintainance fee.

Bank of America Advantage Safe Banking: This account includes keeping existing money presented available money
and allows customers to purchase items with a debit card or with any mobile or online banking with no monthly fee.

Bank of America Advantage Plus Banking: This account offers an option to waive monthly fees, pay with debit card
or with mobile/online banking app. This also allows the option of adding overdraft protection.


Saving Accounts: With Bank of America savings accounts, a minimum of $100 is need to open an account. Monthly
fees can be avoided by maintaining a minimum balance of $500, linking it to a Bank of America Advantage Relationship
Banking account, or if owner is a student under the age of 24.


3. Wells Fargo and Co.

Checking Accounts: Wells Fargo provides a everyday checking account for students between the ages of 17-24. To open
an account, students would have to have a minimum of $25 and will have monthly fees waived. After student passes the
age of 25, the account will be charged a monthly fee of $10 unless one of the following takes place, direct deposit
totaling over $500 or more, 10 or more posted debit card transactions, an $1,500 minimum daily balance or more, or a
linked Wells Fargo campus ATM, or campus debit card. As per the Wells Fargo website, after October 8, 2020, the 10
or more posted debit card transactions will no longer be an option to waive the monthly fee. Another change in their
site, is with the $1,500 minimum daily balance, after October 8, 2020, the amount will be lowered to $500.


Savings Accounts: Wells Fargo offers two main savings accounts.

Wells Fargo Way2Save Savings: This savings account requires a minimum of $25 along with a monthly fee of $25. The
monthly can be avoided by having a daily minimum of $300, one or more transfers from a checking account, automatic
transfer of $25 from a checking account, or the primary owner is under the age of 18. This account include standard
rates of 0.01%.

Platinum Savings: This account requires a minimum of $25 and a monthly fee of $12 that can be avoided by maintaining
a daily minimum of $3,500. This account includes standard rates ranging from 0.01% to 0.02%.


4. Citigroup/Citibank Inc.

Checking Accounts: For checking accounts, Citigroup/Citibank doesn't have a particular account that caters to students,
however, there are basic accounts that may be useful. With basic accounts, they do not have monthly fees.

Basic Banking Account: This account consists of unlimited check writing, seamless money movement, and free ATM
access.


Savings Accounts: With this account, there is no minimum required when opening the account.
A savings account with Citigroup is opened when purchasing a package in which includes the rate, balance, requirements,
benefits, and more. Includes easy transfers between accounts from Citigroup and non Citigroup accounts, has the option
setting balances and deposit alerts, mobile check deposit, higher rates when the balance grows and the option of writing
checks.


5. U.S Bancorp

Checking Accounts: This bank offers online and mobile banking and does cater to student accounts. For some
schools such as colleges and universities, are associated with this bank, which can links a student's identification
that can offer student cards to be able to be used around campus. With a checking account, they do require a $2.00
when waiting to receive paper statement, however e statements, they do not charge a fee. To open an account,
students are required to have a minimum of $25 and does not contain a monthly fee.


Saving Accounts:

Standard Saving: This account requires a minimum of $25 to open an account and includes features such as online
and mobile banking, automated saving options, e- statements, no transaction fees when utilizing a U.S Bancorp ATM,
overdraft protection, and customized alerts. This account has a monthly fee of $0 to $4 and can be avoided by having
a daily minimum balance of $300, monthly collected balance of $1000, or if the primary owner is under the age of 18.
