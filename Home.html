Why is financial literacy so important?

By: Viannie Veras

A teenagers guide to financial success in the future by starting now. In this guide, it will
discuss not only the importance of financial literacy, but components that factor into it such as
credit score, budgeting, managing debt such as student loans, as well as what the top five banks have
to offer when it comes to starting your financial journey.


As teenagers and young adults, it is important to know why we should start our financial journey now.
When obtaining a job and earning money, most people like to spend the majority of that money to seek
acceptance of others by looking cool, but when entering adulthood and attending higher education, it
can become hard balancing life and what may come after it. Starting now can help with stablilty and
can help with avoiding the fear of what's next?


The Basics (Budgeting)

The basics of financial literacy begins with budgeting. Everything revolves around budgeting as it is
very useful for financial success. Starting with a budget helps manage your money efficiently and helps
plan towards a goal. First, you would consider how much you earn, and with that construct a list of expenses
and write out how much they cost. Then you would constuct another list of other expenses such as shopping and
entertainment, then strategize how much you are willing to spend, however still having money to save. After
determining how much you are spending on expenses, consider the surplus and set aside a good portion or what
ever you can to your savings. Try to keep budegting and saving a habit and keep it consistant.

Saving Money

Another aspect of financial literacy is saving money. Saving money is important when it comes to things like emergencies
and financial goals. Saving goes along with budegeting, it is something that needs to be a habit and is something
that needs to be consistant. With consistancy comes gains and accomplishments that will lead you to your financial
dreams. When deciding how much you should start saving for a particular goal, consider time, budget, and surplus.
While working toward a financial or any goal, considering time is crucial, as it determines how long it takes until
it reaches success. This helps with planning even bigger goals that may come up in the future. With this, acknowledging
a budget is also pivotal because a budget plans out your spendings. When having a set plan on spendings, considering
the surplus and decide how much is needed and how much can be put away toward savings. Having a savings plan is very
important because there will be times in life where you don't know what may come next or what may come up and in those
types of situations, having money to be able to fall back on can be extremely helpful.

Retirement Plans

Along with budgeting, comes financial plans. One of the most important financial plans that should be considered is your
retirement plans. Normally, this plan is associated with the benefit of most jobs, as a certain percentage of your paycheck
is required to be put away towards your retirement. In most cases, the percentage deducted from your pay check can be chosen.
In doing so, you would want to make sure that the surplus of your paycheck will suffice your expenses. If you have a job that
does not provide the opportunity to use a percentage of your paycheck to save money for retirement, then set up a savings account,
specifically for retirement and everytime you get paid, set aside a percentage of your paycheck or a set amount of money to save.
With this, ensure that the money being taken out will suffice expenses, as well keeping the payment consistant to make sure the
amount being saved grows over time.

Managing Debt

When working toward a financial goal, such purchasing a house or car, there may be times where you may not have the kind of money
you would like and may need to take out a loan. Loans can be helpful with many things, however, can become a problem if there is no
management of the payments. Mismanagement of a loan can result is debt building upon itself, which can contribute to the decrease in
credit score. It can also take a toll on mental and emotional health as it can cause mental illnesses such as depression and anxiety.
Managing money all starts with budgeting and setting a goal that consists of how much wil be contributed, by when it needs to be payed,
and how long will it takee based off of the amount being payed. With a budget, it can help with the management of debt, expenses, and other
financial plans. It is very important to make sure that before taking out a loan, a plan is already set on how the debt will be payed off
and how long it will take based off the amount being planned to pay.
